# Pattern Intelligence Engine — Methodology

> PIE is a supply chain, regulatory, and competitive intelligence system
> for the UAS ecosystem. This document explains how it works for users
> who want to understand or verify what they're seeing.

---

## What PIE Is

PIE is an automated pipeline that runs daily and produces:

- **Flags** — specific, scored intelligence signals about supply chain
  risks, regulatory changes, gray zone entities, and program developments
- **Brief** — a synthesized daily summary of the top signals
- **Predictions** — probabilistic forecasts for supply chain events
- **Trends** — time-series tracking of key metrics

PIE data is not opinion or analysis. It is derived from:
1. Real-time scraping of 20+ defense and UAS news sources
2. Component pricing and availability from distributor APIs
3. Federal procurement data (SAM.gov, USASpending)
4. Court dockets for regulatory enforcement cases
5. The Forge parts database (3,495 components, 34 categories)

## Flag Types

### `db_supply_constraint`
Component has fewer than 5 NDAA-compliant alternatives in the Forge DB,
or current price is >25% above 30-day average. Signal: supply chain risk.

### `db_landscape`
Category has >60% Chinese-origin parts by count. Signal: dependency risk
for operators needing NDAA compliance.

### `db_supply_chain_risk`
Component is single-source (one manufacturer) or has >16 weeks lead time.
Derived from Forge DB `lead_time_weeks` and manufacturer count per model.

### `db_diversion`
Component confirmed or suspected in Russian weapons systems (per open-source
reporting). Forge DB `tags` field includes `diversion_risk`.

### `db_compliance`
Component has a material NDAA/FCC compliance issue that affects
procurement decisions. Includes FCC Covered List items.

### `market_signal`
Intel article contains a supply, acquisition, funding, or program signal
scored above the relevance threshold. NLP-extracted from article content.

### `gray_zone`
Entity shows multiple indicators of gray zone status: Chinese-derived
hardware, US corporate veneer, or adversary connections. See Gray Zone
scoring methodology below.

### `regulatory`
New regulation, rulemaking, enforcement action, or program change that
affects Blue UAS, NDAA, or BVLOS operations.

### `procurement`
Federal contract award, solicitation, or program signal from SAM.gov or
USASpending above the minimum contract value threshold ($50K).

## Scoring

### Flag Severity

Severity is computed from a combination of:
- **Confidence**: how many independent signals support the flag (0.0–1.0)
- **Impact**: how many platforms/programs are affected
- **Urgency**: time-sensitive (e.g., deadline approaching) vs evergreen

```
CRITICAL  — confidence >0.8 AND (impact >5 platforms OR time-sensitive)
WARNING   — confidence >0.5 OR impact >2 platforms
INFO      — confidence >0.3, low impact
```

### Gray Zone Scoring

Gray zone entities are scored 0.0–1.0 based on:

| Signal | Weight |
|--------|--------|
| Chinese HQ or parent company | 0.3 |
| Identical PCB/firmware to covered entity | 0.25 |
| Active FCC covered list enforcement | 0.2 |
| PRC government investment (verified) | 0.2 |
| US corporate entity only (no manufacturing) | 0.1 |
| NDAA §1709 named | 0.15 |
| Active civil litigation / enforcement | 0.1 |

Score >0.6 = gray zone. Score >0.8 = high-confidence gray zone.

Current high-confidence gray zone entities:
- SkyRover / Knowact (0.86) — identical DJI hardware, Chinese HQ
- Anzu Robotics (0.72) — DJI-derived, US-registered
- Cogito / SPECTA (0.68) — identical DJI Air 3 PCB confirmed

## Prediction Engine

Predictions are generated by an LLM (Claude) that receives:
1. The current flag set with severities and trends
2. Recent intel articles (past 7 days)
3. Historical flag data (past 30 days)
4. Forge DB supply chain metrics

The LLM is prompted to reason about causal chains, not correlations:
"Given flag X and intel article Y, what outcome Z is likely and why?"

Predictions include:
- **Probability** (0.5–0.95 range — below 0.5 not surfaced)
- **Timeframe** (30 days / 60 days / 90 days)
- **Hedge** — explicit uncertainty statement
- **Evidence chain** — what signals support the prediction

Predictions are never presented as facts. The hedge is mandatory.

---

## What PIE Is Not

**PIE is not a recommendation engine.** It surfaces signals; procurement
decisions require independent human judgment and legal review.

**PIE does not validate vendor claims.** Self-attestation documents
are included but marked as such. Verified data from DIU/DCMA takes precedence.

**PIE is not surveillance.** No personally identifiable information is
collected or processed. All analysis is entity-level (companies, programs,
components), not individual-level.

**PIE predictions are probabilistic, not deterministic.** A 77% probability
means 23% chance of being wrong. High-stakes decisions should not rely
on any single prediction.

## Limitations & Known Issues

**Coverage gaps:** Eastern European and Asian-language defense reporting
is underrepresented. Most sources are English-language.

**LLM synthesis errors:** The prediction LLM occasionally generates
plausible-sounding but incorrect causal chains. The hedge statement is
mandatory but not always sufficient.

**Lead time data lag:** Distributor API pricing updates daily, but
actual lead times can diverge from quoted lead times during allocation
events. Treat lead time data as directional, not precise.

**Gray zone scoring is imperfect:** Scores reflect available open-source
evidence. A low gray zone score does not mean an entity is clean —
it may mean less information is available.
